# Galentines 2026: The Love of God Conference

Designing and building an event website for an annual Christian women's conference.

---

## The Project

Galentines Global is an annual conference celebrating faith, sisterhood, and community. For the 2026 edition—themed "The Love of God"—the organizers needed a site that could handle registrations, showcase speakers, and evolve as plans came together.

---

## Design & Aesthetics

The visual design centers on warmth and celebration:

- **Animated hero text** with a rotating image slideshow showcasing moments from past conferences
- **Gradient logo** and cohesive color palette reflecting the conference's feminine, faith-centered brand
- **Scripture verse** integrated into the hero—grounding the experience in purpose
- **Image carousel** highlighting community moments: worship, fellowship, joy
- **Speaker grid** with graceful mobile carousel for smaller screens

Typography and spacing create breathing room. The layout feels inviting before attendees even arrive.

---

## Functionality

Built with Next.js and deployed on Vercel:

- **RSVP system** with Formspree integration—attendees register, receive confirmation emails, and the organizer gets instant notifications
- **Event countdown** displaying days until the conference
- **Live stream button** linking directly to YouTube for remote attendees
- **SEO structured data** (Schema.org Event markup) so the conference appears correctly in Google search results
- **Volunteer & partnership pages** with dedicated forms for community involvement

---

## What Made It Easier for the Client

All content lives in a single `cms.ts` file—event details, page copy, images, speaker info. When the founder needs to:

- **Update the event date**: One line change
- **Swap hero images**: Drop new files, update paths
- **Edit speaker bios**: Modify the ministers array
- **Adjust page copy**: Update the relevant section

No database. No admin dashboard to learn. Just clear, organized data in one place.

---

## Results

The site launched smoothly and served as the central hub for conference communications. Updates happened quickly—no waiting on developer availability. The RSVP flow captured registrations without friction.
